Stars & Nebulae

The brilliant life cycles of stars and the cosmic clouds that shape them.

Birth of Stars

Stars form inside dense molecular clouds where gravity pulls gas and dust together. As the core heats up, nuclear fusion ignites, creating a newborn star that begins shining across the cosmos.

Nebulae

Nebulae are vast clouds of gas and dust. Some are stellar nurseries, while others are the remains of dying stars. Their colors come from glowing ionized gases illuminated by nearby stars.

Stellar Evolution

Stars change dramatically over millions or billions of years. Massive stars explode as supernovae, while smaller stars shed their outer layers to form planetary nebulae, leaving behind white dwarfs.
