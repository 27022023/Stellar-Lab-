Life in the Universe

Exploring the possibility of life beyond Earth — from microbes to intelligent civilizations.

What Life Needs

Scientists search for worlds with liquid water, organic molecules, and energy sources — the essential ingredients for life as we know it.

The Habitable Zone

This is the region around a star where temperatures allow liquid water. But habitability also depends on atmosphere, chemistry, and geology.

Potential Life-Bearing Worlds

Exoplanets, icy moons like Europa and Enceladus, and even Titan’s methane lakes may host environments where life could emerge or survive.

Biosignatures

Oxygen, methane, complex organic molecules, and unusual chemical patterns can hint at biological activity on distant worlds.

The Search for Intelligence

With billions of galaxies and countless planets, intelligent life may exist somewhere. We search using radio signals, spectroscopy, and deep-space missions.
