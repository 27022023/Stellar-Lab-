The Sun, eight planets, dwarf planets, moons, and cosmic wonders.

Solar System Overview

The Sun and all objects orbiting it.

The Solar System formed 4.6 billion years ago from a giant cloud of gas and dust. It contains the Sun, eight planets, dwarf planets, moons, asteroids, comets, and the Kuiper Belt.

⭐ **Key Facts** • Age: 4.6 billion years • Largest planet: Jupiter • Hottest planet: Venus • Coldest planet: Neptune • Most tilted planet: Uranus • Most rings: Saturn

Mercury

The smallest and fastest planet.

Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun and has no atmosphere to trap heat.

⭐ **Key Facts** • Distance from Sun: 57 million km • Temperature: −173°C to 427°C • Surface: heavily cratered • Year length: 88 days

⭐ **Why It Matters** Mercury shows what happens to planets exposed to extreme solar radiation.

Venus

The hottest planet with a toxic atmosphere.

Venus is covered in thick clouds of sulfuric acid and has crushing pressure.

⭐ **Key Facts** • Temperature: 465°C • Atmosphere: CO₂ + sulfuric acid • Pressure: 92× Earth • Rotation: backwards

⭐ **Why It Matters** Venus is a warning of runaway greenhouse effects.

Earth

The only known planet with life.

Earth is the only planet with liquid water on the surface and a protective atmosphere.

⭐ **Key Facts** • Age: 4.5 billion years • Water coverage: 71% • Atmosphere: nitrogen + oxygen • Moon: stabilizes Earth’s tilt

⭐ **Why It Matters** Earth is the blueprint for habitability.

Mars

The Red Planet with potential past life.

Mars has the largest volcano and canyon in the Solar System.

⭐ **Key Facts** • Temperature: −63°C average • Atmosphere: thin CO₂ • Olympus Mons: largest volcano • Water: frozen in poles

⭐ **Why It Matters** Mars may have once supported life.

Jupiter

The largest planet with powerful storms.

Jupiter is a gas giant with a massive storm called the Great Red Spot.

⭐ **Key Facts** • Diameter: 139,820 km • Moons: 95+ • Composition: hydrogen + helium • Great Red Spot: 300-year storm

⭐ **Why It Matters** Jupiter’s gravity shapes the entire Solar System.

Saturn

The planet with the most iconic rings.

Saturn’s rings are made of ice and rock.

⭐ **Key Facts** • Diameter: 116,460 km • Moons: 146+ • Rings: billions of particles • Density: lower than water

⭐ **Why It Matters** Saturn’s rings reveal how planets and moons form.

Uranus

The sideways planet.

Uranus rotates on its side due to a massive ancient collision.

⭐ **Key Facts** • Tilt: 98° • Temperature: −224°C • Type: ice giant • Rings: faint

⭐ **Why It Matters** Uranus shows how violent early Solar System impacts were.

Neptune

The coldest and windiest planet.

Neptune has supersonic winds and a deep blue color from methane.

⭐ **Key Facts** • Temperature: −225°C • Winds: 2,100 km/h • Type: ice giant • Moons: 14

⭐ **Why It Matters** Neptune reveals extreme weather in the outer Solar System.

Dwarf Planets

Small worlds beyond Neptune.

Dwarf planets include Pluto, Eris, Haumea, Makemake, and Ceres.

⭐ **Key Facts** • Located in Kuiper Belt or asteroid belt • Some have moons • Pluto has mountains of ice • Haumea is oval-shaped

⭐ **Why They Matter** Dwarf planets show the diversity of small worlds.
